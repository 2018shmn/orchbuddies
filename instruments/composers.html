Groundbreaking Composers

Florence Price

Price became the first African-American female composer to have a composition performed by a major American orchestra. In 1933, the Chicago Symphony Orchestra played the world premiere of her Symphony No. 1 in E minor.

    • William Grant Still

    Still was the first African-American composer to have a symphony performed by a professional orchestra in the U.S. His Symphony no. 1 "Afro-American" was premiered by the Rochester Philharmonic in 1931.
